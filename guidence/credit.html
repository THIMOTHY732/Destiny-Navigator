Credit Analyst

Description

Assesses creditworthiness of individuals, companies, and institutions. Collaborates with other financial experts to approve or deny loans. Makes recommendations about whether to increase, adjust, extend, or close lines of credit.

Career Path

Educational Qualifications A credit analyst is required to obtain a bachelor's degree in business, finance or accounting, or at least an associated degree and relevant experience in a financial institution.
Practical Experience Bank Operations Internship, Entry-Level Bank Roles, Advancement Opportunities
Key Qualities Decision Making, Communication, Leadership, Financial Acumen

The average salary for Credit Analyst is ₹9,16,142 per year in the India. The average additional cash compensation for a Credit Analyst in the India is ₹72,204, with a range from ₹69,556 - ₹74,852.
